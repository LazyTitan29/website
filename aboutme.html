THE HISTORY OF OUR TRADITIONAL MEXICAN RESTAURANT:

It all began in 2015 when Mexican Foor for the Hungry opened their first restaurant, in City of Malolos Bulacan. That day, they established their commitment to quality ingredients served with a warm and friendly touch – a commitment that continues today as we celebrate our 5th anniversary! The successful restaurant has now grown as Mexican Food For Hungry and it has two branches in Bulacan. Still the prominent friends -owned and locally- operated, they serve traditional Mexican food with no compromises. Today, the staff of the said Mexican restaurant is run by one of the relatives of the owners. Here at Lutong Mexican sa Inang Bayan, they focus on community involvement and fresh Mexican cuisine, growing their own farm-fresh chilies, blending them into delicious signature recipes and delivering each meal with unequaled hospitality. You will taste the tradition at every table, in every dish, and within every savory ingredient! Every family has their secrets – ours just happen to be recipes.

OUR CLIENT

Simple Rules of Good Restaurant Customer Service: An integral part of any restaurant is its customer service. It doesn’t matter how fabulous your restaurant décor is or how delicious your food is, if the service doesn’t meet or exceed customers’ expectations, there is a good chance they won’t come back. Customer service covers many different parts of a restaurant's day to day operations, going far beyond the front of the house staff The Customer Is Always Right: It is the golden rule of business. Even if you think the customer is wrong, you never, ever, tell them that. Be understanding and empathetic and take the time to listen to their complaint or suggestion. Problems will inevitably arise in even the best of restaurants - it’s how you handle them that will determine if customer return or go elsewhere. Customers are the greatest asset for any business and no matter, right or wrong, the business cannot do without them; they might not be hundred percent right all of the time, but they should still be given a benefit of doubt. Customers need to feel pleased, feel like an entity and it is extremely necessary to personally know your customers and listen to them. Always remember that the first impression is the last impression, thus handling customers with a great attitude will be auspicious and empowering for the business . “Empowering customers can take place by providing them with sufficient knowledge or the nature of business and their products.” However every rule has its exceptions, so does this one. So always treat the customers’ right.
